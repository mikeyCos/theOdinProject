Ingredients

Chips

  • 3 Russet potatoes
  • 2 Tablespoons Kosher salt
  • 1/2 Tablespoon black pepper
  • 1/4 Cup extra-virgin olive oil

Fish

  • 1 1/2 Pounds fresh cod filet; cut into 4-inch pieces
  • 1 1/2 Cups all-purpose flour
  • 5 Tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 Teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 Teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 Teaspoon gralic powder
  • 1 3/4 Cup sparkling water
  • 1/2 Bunch fresh parsley
  • 2-3 Lemons; cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F to roast the potatoes.
  2. Place the potatoes onto a baking sheet, cover in salt, pepper, and oil. Roat in oven for about 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the potatoes from the oven and cut them into large wedges and let them cool.
  4. Start by frying the potatoes that have been cut into wedges. Place the potatoes into the fryer for 4 minutes gently stirring occasionally to make sure they are not sticking together or to the sides or bottom of the fryer. You may need to fry in batches depending on the size of your fryer.
  5. Remove the potatoes from the fryer and place them on a tray lined with paper towels for about 10 minutes to cool off. Once cooled, return the potatoes to the fryer and cook another 5-6 minutes until dark golden brown and crispy. Place the potatoes on a tray lined with paper towels and season with salt.
  6. Dip the dried-off pieces of fish into the batter, making sure to evenly coat each side. Place the fish pieces into the fryer and cook for 8 1/2 miutes or until dark golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the fish fromt je fryer and place on a tray lined with paper towels. Season with salt.
  8. After all the potatoes and fish have been cooked, fry the parsley leaves in the hot oil and set aside.
  9. To serve, place the fish and chips onto a large platter and garnish with lemon wedges, fried parsley, salt, and your choice of sauce.
